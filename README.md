## C# Tutoring: Free Platforms to Advertise Your Services

To help you get started as a C# tutor, here is a curated list of free locations and platforms where you can advertise your services and find students.

---

## 1. Tutoring Marketplaces (High Traffic)
These platforms allow you to create a tutor profile for free. They handle the search infrastructure so students can find you.

* **[FindTutors](https://www.findtutors.co.uk/become-a-tutor)**
    * **Best for:** Keeping your full fee.
    * **Note:** They currently offer a 0% commission model.
* **[Superprof](https://www.superprof.co.uk)**
    * **Best for:** Global reach and ease of use.
    * **Note:** It is free to list; students usually pay a fee to unlock your contact details.
* **[Tutorful](https://tutorful.co.uk/become-a-tutor)**
    * **Best for:** Professionalism and built-in virtual classrooms.
    * **Note:** They handle the admin and billing in exchange for a service fee.
* **[Tutor Hunt](https://www.tutorhunt.com)**
    * **Best for:** Direct communication with local and online students.

---

## 2. Developer Communities (The "Soft Sell")
Build authority and find students where they already hang out.

* **Discord:** Join servers like the [C# Discord](https://discord.gg/csharp) or [DotNet](https://discord.gg/dotnet). Look for `#community-help` or `#job-boards`.
* **Reddit:**
    * [r/ProgrammingBuddies](https://www.reddit.com/r/ProgrammingBuddies/): Post a "Mentor" offering.
    * [r/learncsharp](https://www.reddit.com/r/learncsharp/): Help people with questions and offer 1-on-1 sessions.
    * [r/TutorsForHire](https://www.reddit.com/r/TutorsForHire/): A dedicated marketplace for tutoring ads.
* **LinkedIn:** Use the "Providing Services" feature on your profile. Share small C# tips to demonstrate your teaching style.

---

## 3. Local Advertising
* **[Nextdoor](https://nextdoor.co.uk/):** Great for finding local students or parents looking for coding tutors for their children.
* **Facebook Groups:** Search for "Computer Science Students" or "University of [City] Tutors."

---

## Tips for Your Advertisement
* **Highlight your Niche:** Are you teaching **Unity Game Development**, **ASP.NET Core Web APIs**, or **Basic Console Apps**?
* **Show, Don't Just Tell:** Mention your experience with the .NET ecosystem (C# 12/13, .NET 9/10).
* **The First Session:** Offer a free 15-minute "Intro/Setup" session to ensure your development environments (VS Code on Ubuntu vs. Visual Studio on Windows) are compatible.
